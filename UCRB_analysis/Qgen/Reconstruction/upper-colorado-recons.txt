ABSTRACT:
Updated proxy reconstructions of water year (October-September) streamflow 
for four key gauges in the Upper Colorado River Basin were generated using 
an expanded tree ring network and longer calibration records than in previous 
efforts. Reconstructed gauges include the Green River at Green River, Utah; 
Colorado near Cisco, Utah; San Juan near Bluff, Utah; and Colorado at 
Lees Ferry, Arizona. The reconstructions explain 72-81% of the variance in the 
gauge records, and results are robust across several reconstruction approaches. 
Time series plots as well as results of cross-spectral analysis indicate strong 
spatial coherence in runoff variations across the subbasins. The Lees Ferry 
reconstruction suggests a higher long-term mean than previous reconstructions
but strongly supports earlier findings that Colorado River allocations were 
based on one of the wettest periods in the past 5 centuries and that droughts 
more severe than any 20th to 21st century event occurred in the past.

GEOGRAPHIC REGION: Western North America, Colorado River Basin
PERIOD OF RECORD:  1490 - 2000 AD. Individual gage reconstructions begin 
                   between 1490 and 1569 AD, and end between 1997 and 2000 AD.

DESCRIPTION:  
Updated Streamflow Reconstructions for the Upper Colorado River Basin, based on
a network of moisture-sensitive tree-ring chronologies in Colorado, southwestern
Wyoming, and northeastern Utah.

Tree-ring base reconstructions were generated for a set of 10 gages in the Upper 
Colorado River basin including Lees Ferry, as well as gages in the Green, Gunnison, 
Colorado main stem, and San Juan River basins.  Reconstructions were generated using 
several reconstruction methods to test the robustness of results to different 
statistical approaches.  All the gages but Lees Ferry were generated using both a full 
set of predictor chronologies and a geographically limited (upstream watershed + 
100km buffer) set with stepwise regression.  Four different approaches were used for 
Lees Ferry; residual chronologies in stepwise regression (Lees-A), standard chronologies 
in stepwise regression (Lees-B), principal components regression with residual 
chronologies (Lees-C), and principal components regression with standard chronologies 
(Lees-D). Units are millions of cubic meters.  Details on methodology are in 
Woodhouse et al. 2006.

The natural flow estimates for the gage data, 1906-1995, used for the calibrations 
were provided by Jim Prairie of the U.S. Bureau of Reclamation in the summer of 2005. 
Units are in millions of cubic meters.

1. Full Pool Reconstructions for 9 gage locations
2. Geographically-Limited Pool  Reconstructions for 9 gage locations
3. Lees Ferry Reconstructions, 4 alternative methods
4. Gage Data (instrumental)
